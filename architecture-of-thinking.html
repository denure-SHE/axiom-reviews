A leader navigating the intersection of technology, risk, and organizational decision-making discovers that the hardest questions about intelligent systems are not technical — they are institutional, ethical, and deeply human. Told in parallel across the boardroom and the kitchen table, this novel traces what it means to govern reasoning itself.

Themes & Ideas

The Architecture of Reasoning How do organizations build systems that think? And who is responsible when the order of reasoning inside those systems begins to shift?
Signal vs. Noise The financial risk engine flags breaches — but Ananya is troubled by what it almost misses. Threshold-based detection versus pattern recognition as a governance problem.
Parallel Worlds The boardroom and the kitchen table ask the same questions. When should you intervene? When do you step back? What does leadership look like when the goal is trust, not control?
